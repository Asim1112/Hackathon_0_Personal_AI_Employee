## Post Content

Most professional services firms don't have an automation problem. They have an oversight problem.

The fear isn't that AI will get something wrong. The fear is that it gets something wrong and nobody notices until it's already caused damage — to a client relationship, an invoice, or a reputation.

That's why the AI Employee is built around a Human-in-the-Loop architecture from the ground up. Here's what that looks like in practice:

A client email arrives. The AI triages, classifies priority, and drafts a reply. You review and approve before anything is sent.

A LinkedIn enquiry comes in from a high-value prospect. The AI assesses the fit, drafts a response, and flags it for you. You decide whether to engage.

An invoice goes 30+ days overdue. The AI drafts a payment chase and creates the Odoo record. You sign off before any money conversation happens.

Five channels — Email, LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Odoo — running simultaneously. Every draft visible. Every action logged. Nothing autonomous.

For the firms I work with, that's not a limitation. That's the only way AI-assisted operations ever gets past the board.

If you're running a professional services business and spending hours on operational overhead that AI could be handling — I'd be glad to talk through what this looks like in your context.
