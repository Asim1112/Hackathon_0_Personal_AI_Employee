Full automation sounds great until you're signing off on an email you didn't write to a client you've worked with for three years.

Most AI tools for business communications operate on a binary: fully automated or fully manual. Neither works in professional services. Fully automated systems expose you to reputational risk — one wrong email to a key client can undo years of relationship-building. Fully manual means you're still doing two to three hours of routine triage every morning.

The approach that actually works is Human-in-the-Loop: the AI drafts, classifies, and prepares everything — and you approve before anything is sent. Email replies, LinkedIn posts, invoice creation, payment chases. The system monitors five channels simultaneously (email, LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Odoo ERP), applies your business rules to every item, and queues everything for a single human review point. You spend five minutes approving, not five hours doing.

The critical insight is that this is not about replacing judgement. It's about removing the cognitive load that prevents judgement from being applied where it actually matters — client relationships, strategic decisions, business development.

If you run a professional services firm and want to see how this works in practice, I'm happy to walk through a live demo. The system is running now.
