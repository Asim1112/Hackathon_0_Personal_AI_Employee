**The hidden cost of manual operations in professional services is rarely the time — it's the decisions that don't get made because your team is buried in emails.**

Most firms we speak to have the same problem. Senior people spending two to three hours a day on email triage, invoice chasing, and drafting routine correspondence. It's not that the work is complex — it's that it never stops, and it crowds out the higher-value thinking that actually drives client outcomes.

The question we get asked most often isn't "can AI do this?" — it's "how do we maintain oversight when AI is involved?" That's the right question. Full automation is a governance risk for most professional services firms. What works is a Human-in-the-Loop approach: AI handles the monitoring, classification, and drafting; humans retain control over what gets sent, posted, or invoiced.

What that looks like in practice: five live channels monitored simultaneously (email, LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Odoo ERP), every response drafted automatically, nothing executed without explicit human approval, and a full audit trail on every action. The overhead of oversight is minutes per day, not hours.

If your team is carrying that operational weight and wondering whether there's a better model — it's worth a conversation.
