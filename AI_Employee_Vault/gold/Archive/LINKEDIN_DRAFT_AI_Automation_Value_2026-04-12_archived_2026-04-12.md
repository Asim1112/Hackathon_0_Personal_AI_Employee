## Draft Post

Most professional services firms that evaluate AI automation make the same mistake: they optimise for removing the human from the process.

The result is a system that nobody trusts — and in regulated industries, a process that runs without oversight isn't an asset, it's a liability.

The systems that actually get deployed share one characteristic: the human remains in the loop at every decision point that matters. The AI reads, classifies, and drafts. The human decides what goes out. Every action is logged. Nothing executes without a record.

That's not a limitation. It's the architecture that earns internal sign-off.

For professional services businesses dealing with email overload, manual invoice chasing, and thought leadership content that never gets written — the question isn't whether to automate. It's whether the automation is trustworthy enough for client-facing work.

If you're evaluating AI for your operations, ask one question first: what happens when it's wrong? The answer tells you everything about whether it's safe to deploy.
