**Your operations team is spending 15 hours a week on tasks an AI Employee could draft in minutes.**

That is not an exaggeration. For a professional services firm of 40–50 people, the cumulative cost of manual email triage, overdue invoice chasing, and thought leadership that never gets written is typically £60,000–£90,000 per year in lost billable time. Not because the work is hard — because it is repetitive, and repetitive work does not get prioritised when client deadlines are pressing.

The systems that address this tend to fail at one of two points: they automate too aggressively (which means errors go out in your name without your knowledge), or they create more overhead than they save (another dashboard to check, another tool to manage).

The approach that works is narrower than most vendors want to admit. An AI system that monitors your inboxes, drafts responses, flags overdue payments and queues posts — but executes nothing without explicit human sign-off. Every action audited. Every approval documented. Nothing sent, posted or invoiced until a person confirms it.

This is not about replacing judgement. It is about making sure your judgement is applied to the decisions that actually matter, rather than spent on the ones that do not.

If you are running a professional services business and operational overhead is the thing slowing you down, this is a conversation worth having.
