**Most projects don't fail because of bad ideas. They fail because the right expertise wasn't in the room early enough.**

Here is a pattern I see regularly in business: a team works hard on a project for months, encounters a critical problem at the worst possible moment, and only then brings in outside expertise to help untangle it.

The irony? A few well-placed conversations at the start — with someone who has navigated similar challenges — could have prevented the problem entirely, or at least made the path forward far clearer.

Expert consulting isn't about handing over control of your project. It's about giving your team the perspective and tools they need to succeed faster and with greater confidence. The best outcomes happen when internal capability and external expertise work together from day one.

If you are working on something complex and want a fresh perspective, I would love to hear about it.

What's the best thing outside expertise has done for a project you've worked on? Let me know in the comments.
